Some history about the Chihuahua breed

The Chihuahua, named for the Mexican state of Chihuahua, is currently recognized as the smallest dog breed. The Chihuahua is thought to
related to the Techichi, which was a small dog kept by the Toltec people. Chihuahuas tend to be 5"-8" tall, and generally weigh from 1-6 pounds.
They have varying color with hair that is either smooth/shiny or long/soft. The Chihuahua was formally recognized by the
American Kennel Club in 1904.

A few facts about the Chihuahua breed

  • Chihuahuas need exercise and love daily walks.
  • Due to their size, they are vulnerable to larger dogs and birds of prey
  • They love to play with small toys, but are not as excited about retrieving
  • Due to their large body surface area to volume ratio, they tend to lose a lot of body heat
  • Chihuahuas are great watchdogs, but generally ineffective protection dogs
  • Their lifespan averages about 15-16 years

Additional fun facts

  1. Chihuahuas love to be cozy and will bury themselves in blankets
  2. Chihuahuas are often good dogs for apartment dwellers and/or the elderly
  3. Chihuahuas have varying personality traits and can be quite entertaining to watch
